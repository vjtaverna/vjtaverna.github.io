Pitt Makerspace Workshops

The Inspiration

If you’ve been directed to my website by my resume, you may have seen the project labelled “12-Key MacroPad.” This was a basic project that I embarked on over the Summer of 2023 to begin exploring human interface devices (HIDs), how to design them, and how to code them. I found a sweet Github repo that goes through a basic design and code of a macropad. Over the following months, I learned how to use EAGLE to design and manufacture a PCB for use in the project. After some minor setbacks (Pitt’s makerspace couldn’t manufacture my PCB, so I went through JLC), I had a functioning board.

The Execution

I worked with a friend of mine to interface my PCB and microcontroller with some upgraded code. You can find the Github repo here. After this project concluded, I was approached by my friend Drew about working with the Pitt Makerspace to walk students through a similar project. I happily obliged. We put together a 3 session workshop that took students through the design of the PCB, casting of resin keycaps, and soldering/coding of their board. Overall, the workshop was a massive success! We had about 20 students attend all 3 workshops to build their macropads. You can find a visual tour of the workshop below.

Good Followup

After the workshop, I wanted to make sure that students had a really thorough understanding of the code, since we ran short on time. As a result, I recorded a YouTube video of me walking through code line by line. Overall, I had a really good time with this project, and I hope to keep exploring HIDs in the future.
